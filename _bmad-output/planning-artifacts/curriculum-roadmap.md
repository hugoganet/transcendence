# Curriculum Roadmap — transcendence

## Overview

**Total:** 6 categories, 18 chapters, 69 missions
**Mission duration:** 2-5 minutes each
**Total learning time:** ~5-6 hours
**Target completion rate:** >40% (vs industry 5-35%)

### Exercise Types

- **IP** = Interactive Placement (drag-and-drop, spatial/tactile)
- **CM** = Concept Matching (categorical, vocabulary, mental models)
- **ST** = Step-by-Step Simulated Transaction (sequential/procedural)
- **SI** = Scenario-Based Interpretation (analytical, real-world application)

### Progressive Reveal Alignment

| Moment | Mission | What Unlocks |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Tokens revealed | 2.2.4 "Your Knowledge Tokens" | Knowledge Tokens visible in UI |
| Wallet-profile unlocked | 3.1.4 "Set Up Your First Wallet" | Profile becomes wallet interface |
| Gas-fee mechanic activated | 3.3.3 "Gas in Action" | Platform gas costs begin |
| Full dashboard | 6.3.4 "Graduation" | Complete stats + certificate |

## Category 1: Blockchain Foundations

**Core question:** "What is this technology and why does it exist?"
**Platform mechanic:** XP only (baseline)

### Chapter 1.1 — The Trust Problem

| # | Mission | Learning Objective | Type |
|---|---------|-------------------|------|
| 1.1.1 | **Who Do You Trust?** | Understand that most daily transactions rely on middlemen (banks, PayPal, notaries) | SI |
| 1.1.2 | **What Could Go Wrong?** | Understand single points of failure — what happens when the middleman fails | SI |
| 1.1.3 | **A World Without Middlemen** | Understand the core value proposition of blockchain: peer-to-peer trust | CM |

**Chapter arc:** Emotional hook → problem awareness → solution preview. No jargon yet. Pure motivation.

### Chapter 1.2 — Blocks, Chains & Consensus

| # | Mission | Learning Objective | Type |
|---|---------|-------------------|------|
| 1.2.1 | **What is a Block?** | Understand that a block is a container of data (transactions) with a timestamp and ID | IP |
| 1.2.2 | **Chaining Blocks Together** | Understand how blocks link via hashes and why changing one breaks the chain | IP |
| 1.2.3 | **Copies Everywhere** | Understand distributed ledger — thousands of identical copies, no single owner | CM |
| 1.2.4 | **How the Network Agrees** | Understand consensus basics — nodes vote on valid transactions (PoW/PoS as analogies) | SI |
| 1.2.5 | **Blockchain vs. Database** | Consolidate understanding — what makes blockchain different from a regular database | CM |

**Chapter arc:** Build a block → chain blocks → distribute them → agree on truth → compare to what you know. The "build then break" mission (1.2.2) is the signature moment — physically seeing the chain break creates visceral understanding.

### Chapter 1.3 — Why Decentralization Matters

| # | Mission | Learning Objective | Type |
|---|---------|-------------------|------|
| 1.3.1 | **Decentralization in Real Life** | Connect decentralization to familiar systems (Wikipedia, torrents, farmers markets) | CM |
| 1.3.2 | **Who Uses Blockchain Today?** | Understand real-world adoption — supply chain, healthcare records, voting pilots | SI |
| 1.3.3 | **Is Blockchain Always the Answer?** | Develop critical thinking — blockchain isn't magic, it has trade-offs | SI |

**Chapter arc:** Ground it in reality → show real adoption → build critical thinking. Mission 1.3.3 prevents the "blockchain solves everything" naive enthusiasm and builds trust with anti-crypto-bro positioning.

## Category 2: Crypto & Tokens

**Core question:** "What are digital currencies and tokens?"
**Platform mechanic unlocked:** Knowledge Tokens revealed (mission 2.2.4)

### Chapter 2.1 — Digital Money: A New Kind of Currency

| # | Mission | Learning Objective | Type |
|---|---------|-------------------|------|
| 2.1.1 | **The Satoshi Story** | Understand Bitcoin's origin — 2008 crisis, the whitepaper, pseudonymous creator | SI |
| 2.1.2 | **Digital Money vs. Your Bank Account** | Understand how crypto differs from digital euros in your banking app | CM |
| 2.1.3 | **Where Does Crypto Live?** | Understand that crypto exists on blockchains, not on servers or in files | IP |
| 2.1.4 | **Why Would Anyone Use This?** | Understand the value proposition — borderless, permissionless, 24/7, programmable | SI |

**Chapter arc:** Story hook → comparison to familiar → where it lives → why it matters. Opens with Satoshi as a narrative hook.

### Chapter 2.2 — Coins, Tokens & the Crypto Zoo

| # | Mission | Learning Objective | Type |
|---|---------|-------------------|------|
| 2.2.1 | **Coins vs. Tokens** | Understand the difference — coins have their own blockchain, tokens live on another's | CM |
| 2.2.2 | **Ethereum: The World Computer** | Understand Ethereum's role — not just money but a platform for building things | SI |
| 2.2.3 | **The Token Universe** | Understand token types — utility, governance, stablecoins (simplified) | IP |
| 2.2.4 | **Your Knowledge Tokens** | Understand that transcendence's Knowledge Tokens mirror real token mechanics | CM |

**Chapter arc:** Taxonomy → the platform that made tokens possible → token types → meta-moment connecting the platform to reality.

**Progressive reveal:** Mission 2.2.4 is THE moment. The user learns about tokens, and their Knowledge Tokens become visible in the UI.

### Chapter 2.3 — Value, Price & Volatility

| # | Mission | Learning Objective | Type |
|---|---------|-------------------|------|
| 2.3.1 | **Why Does Crypto Have Value?** | Understand value drivers — scarcity, utility, network effects, belief | CM |
| 2.3.2 | **Reading a Price Chart** | Understand basic price chart elements — not trading, just literacy | IP |
| 2.3.3 | **Why Prices Swing So Much** | Understand volatility — low liquidity, speculation, news impact, market cycles | SI |
| 2.3.4 | **The Investment Disclaimer** | Understand that education ≠ financial advice, and recognize the difference | SI |

**Chapter arc:** Why value → how to read → why volatile → critical thinking about advice. Mission 2.3.4 directly supports FR50 (financial disclaimer) and builds scam resistance.

## Category 3: Wallets & Gas

**Core question:** "How do you store and move digital assets?"
**Platform mechanics unlocked:** Wallet-profile (3.1.4), Gas-fee mechanic (3.3.3)

### Chapter 3.1 — Your Digital Wallet

| # | Mission | Learning Objective | Type |
|---|---------|-------------------|------|
| 3.1.1 | **What is a Crypto Wallet?** | Understand that a wallet doesn't "hold" crypto — it holds keys that prove ownership | CM |
| 3.1.2 | **Public Key, Private Key** | Understand the key pair — public key = address (mailbox), private key = password (mailbox key) | IP |
| 3.1.3 | **Hot Wallets vs. Cold Wallets** | Understand the trade-off — convenience (hot) vs security (cold) | CM |
| 3.1.4 | **Set Up Your First Wallet** | Experience the wallet creation flow in a safe simulation | ST |

**Chapter arc:** What it is → how keys work → types → do it yourself.

**Progressive reveal:** Wallet-profile unlocks after 3.1.4. The user's profile page transforms into a wallet interface.

### Chapter 3.2 — Making a Transaction

| # | Mission | Learning Objective | Type |
|---|---------|-------------------|------|
| 3.2.1 | **Anatomy of a Transaction** | Understand what a crypto transaction contains (sender, recipient, amount, fee, signature) | IP |
| 3.2.2 | **Sending Your First Crypto** | Experience sending crypto step-by-step in simulation | ST |
| 3.2.3 | **Waiting for Confirmation** | Understand confirmation times, mempool, and why transactions aren't instant | SI |
| 3.2.4 | **Transaction on the Blockchain** | Understand that every transaction is permanently recorded and publicly viewable | ST |

**Chapter arc:** What's inside → do it → wait for it → verify it. The full lifecycle.

### Chapter 3.3 — Gas Fees: The Cost of Doing Anything

| # | Mission | Learning Objective | Type |
|---|---------|-------------------|------|
| 3.3.1 | **What is Gas?** | Understand gas as the computational cost of doing anything on a blockchain | CM |
| 3.3.2 | **Why Gas Prices Change** | Understand supply/demand for block space — busy network = expensive gas | SI |
| 3.3.3 | **Gas in Action** | Experience gas fees firsthand in a simulated multi-step operation | ST |
| 3.3.4 | **Oops — Failed Transaction, Gas Still Paid** | Understand that failed transactions still consume gas | SI |

**Chapter arc:** What it is → why it varies → feel it → learn the hard lesson.

**Progressive reveal:** Gas-fee mechanic activates during or after this chapter. The platform's existing mechanic suddenly has full context.

### Chapter 3.4 — Staying Safe

| # | Mission | Learning Objective | Type |
|---|---------|-------------------|------|
| 3.4.1 | **Your Seed Phrase is Everything** | Understand seed phrases — what they are, why they must never be shared | ST |
| 3.4.2 | **Spot the Scam** | Recognize common crypto scam patterns (phishing, fake airdrops, rug pulls) | SI |
| 3.4.3 | **Security Best Practices** | Know the actionable rules — 2FA, hardware wallets, verify URLs, never share keys | CM |
| 3.4.4 | **The Security Checklist** | Consolidate all safety knowledge into a personal checklist | IP |

**Chapter arc:** Most critical secret → recognize dangers → know the rules → internalize as habit. This chapter directly addresses Sarah's fear and builds trust.
